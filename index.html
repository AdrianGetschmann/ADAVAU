Structural driver: The supply shortfall is the single most important reason German residential rents and prices are rising. Regulatory Guide

Key Regulations for Investors

RegulationWhat It MeansWho Is AffectedInvestor Impact
Mietpreisbremse
Rent Brake (until 2029)		 New tenancy rents cannot exceed 10% above local reference rent (Mietspiegel) in designated tight markets All landlords in ~50% of municipalities Limits rental upside on re-letting; caps yield at local market level
Kappungsgrenze
Cap on Increases		 Max 20% rent increase over 3 years (15% in tight markets) for existing tenants All existing tenancies Slows rent catch-up on underrented stock
Neubau Exemption New builds and first lettings post-substantial renovation are fully exempt from Mietpreisbremse New-build buyers Can charge market rent freely; key advantage for new build investors
Indexmietvertrag Lease tied to CPI inflation index — annual increases track official inflation automatically Available to all landlords Popular with investors; under 2026 regulatory review in tight markets
Möblierungszuschlag
Furnished Premium		 Landlords can charge premium for furnished apartments; 2026 draft law requires itemised justification Furnished apartment landlords 20–30% premium currently; regulatory risk growing
Spekulationssteuer
Capital Gains Tax		 Capital gains on sale are fully taxable if property sold within 10 years of purchase All property investors Hold for 10+ years to avoid CGT; plan exit accordingly
