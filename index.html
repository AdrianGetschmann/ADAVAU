Tax & Depreciation (AfA)

In Germany, rental income is taxed at your marginal rate — but you can deduct depreciation (AfA) and mortgage interest, often creating a net tax saving in early years.

Gross Yield
—
Annual rent ÷ purchase price. Above 4% is healthy in Germany; below 3% is difficult to make work with a mortgage.
Net Yield
—
Rent after vacancy, management & maintenance costs ÷ price. This is your real income rate before financing. Above 3% is solid.
Kaufpreisfaktor
—
How many years of rent equals the purchase price. Lower = better value. Under 25× is good; over 35× means you're paying a lot for each euro of rent.
Monthly Cash Flow
—
Cash left each month after all running costs and the mortgage payment. Positive = the property pays for itself from day one.
Cash-on-Cash Return
—
Annual cash flow ÷ the cash you actually put in. This is your "dividend" on the money you invested. 5%+ beats most savings accounts.
Annualised ROI (IRR)
—
True annual return over the full loan term — including rent, appreciation, and selling at the end. 7%+ competes with stock market returns.

Regulatory Guide

Key Regulations for Investors

RegulationWhat It MeansInvestor Impact
Mietpreisbremse
Rent Brake (until 2029)		New tenancy rents cannot exceed 10% above local reference rent in designated tight marketsLimits rental upside on re-letting
KappungsgrenzeMax 20% rent increase over 3 years (15% in tight markets) for existing tenantsSlows rent catch-up on underrented stock
Neubau ExemptionNew builds and first lettings post-substantial renovation are fully exemptCan charge market rent freely
SpekulationssteuerCapital gains on sale are fully taxable if sold within 10 years of purchaseHold for 10+ years to avoid CGT
AfA Depreciation2% (existing) or 3% (new build) of building value deductible from taxable rental income per yearSignificant tax saving, especially in early years
